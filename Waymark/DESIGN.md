# Waymark — Design

*A calm, cinematic command center for the projects that make up a life.*

---

## Who this build is for

**This version is for me, on my own Mac.** Local-only, single-user, no accounts,
no cloud, no network. Whether Waymark becomes a wider product is a **later
decision — made by living with it**, not guessed now.

## Purpose & philosophy

Waymark answers one question each day: **given everything on my plate, what
actually deserves my focus right now?** It cuts through competing priorities —
paid work, long-term goals, creative projects, family — and surfaces the few
things that genuinely matter today, with the reasoning shown so I trust it
instead of re-deciding every morning.

It optimizes for **importance to my real goals**, not throughput. Guiding
principle: **the goal is a good life now, not once all the work is finally
done.** That point never arrives, so Waymark helps me do what matters and then
**stop** — it recognizes "enough for today" and says so. It never nags, never
rewards busyness, and is meant to be calmly ignorable once the day's real work
is handled. **Resting is a valid way to win the day.**

## The three people it's designed for (a design lens)

1. **Me** — many concurrent creative + work projects.
2. **A person with ADHD** — lots of interests, easily distracted, gets stuck on
   *direction*. Needs near-zero friction and one focal thing on screen.
3. **A stay-at-home parent with side projects** — wants to feel *real* progress
   in the margins of a full day.

Design for the ADHD case and it works for all three: open the app and instantly
know the single next step.

## Goals, quests, next steps

- **Goals** are the user's real life goals, **in their own words** (work, money,
  family, health, a creative dream — importance is theirs to define and is not
  limited to career). Defined during onboarding; editable any time.
- **Quests** are projects. Each is tied to a goal (the *why*) and carries a
  single concrete **next step** (the *how*) — because a project-sized goal is
  paralyzing while a clear next step is something you can begin.
- **Long-game progress** is made visible per goal: each goal *grows* (tiers)
  as meaningful milestones land, and a goal with live quests that has gone quiet
  for two weeks is gently flagged ("needs you"). This is the "is my attention
  landing where my goals are" signal — continuous, never a nag. Progress comes
  from **meaningful advancement** (a step done, a stage advanced), never from
  counting tasks or chasing streaks.

## Ranking

Blend **deadline urgency** with **importance**; importance leads, urgency makes
things *today*-relevant. Surface a **short** list — usually one focal step;
runners-up hidden behind a deliberate tap. Each focal item has **one
plain-sentence reason**. Never rank by task count or "quickest to clear."

## First-run

A guided, one-screen-at-a-time intro that explains the *why* before asking for
anything: what Waymark is → the three ideas (Goals, Quests, Today) → name your
goals → add a first quest. Re-runnable from Goals → Replay intro.

## Aesthetic

Calm and **cinematic**: a light, misty, layered atmosphere with soft light and a
gentle vignette, **frosted-glass panels** floating with depth, elegant
sans-serif, generous space — one clear thing in view at a time. The backdrop is
the built-in atmosphere by default, or **the user's own photo** (Goals →
Appearance → Choose background). A deep/moody variant is an easy future toggle.

## Views

1. **Today** — the soul. The single next step worth doing, its reason, one
   action; the Enough state once the important thing is done.
2. **Quests** — every quest grouped by goal; add / edit / advance.
3. **Goals** — the long game: each goal growing, neglect flagged; plus
   appearance settings.

## Later, by living with it

Weekly review · customizable stages · a menu-bar quick-glance · reading a
separate timer app's hours · a deep/moody theme. Kept out to stay tight.
